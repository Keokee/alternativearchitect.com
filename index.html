For over a hundred years America has had an indigenous architecture as the torch of creative imagination and sensitivity has passed from the great pioneers - Louis Sullivan to Frank Lloyd Wright to Bruce Goff - and on to the next generation of architects who adhere to the principles of organic architecture.

Joseph Henry Wythe is one of those architects advancing the cause of organic architecture and "green building," dedicated to the education of coming generations in the delights of an Alternative Architecture.
